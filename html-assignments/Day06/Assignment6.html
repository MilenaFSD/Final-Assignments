Georgian traditional guest

Traditional Georgian Supra is a festive and rich traditional feast that brings family and friends together. It is more than just a meal — it is a cultural celebration full of delicious dishes, heartfelt toasts, and joyful music. At a Supra, the "Tamada" (toastmaster) leads the table, offering meaningful toasts that honor friendship, history, and life itself. Georgian cuisine served at the Supra includes famous dishes like khachapuri (cheese bread), khinkali (dumplings), and various fresh salads and grilled meats. The Supra is a symbol of Georgian hospitality, unity, and deep respect for tradition.

Khinkali is a traditional Georgian dumpling filled with juicy meat, herbs, and spices. It is a beloved dish known for its unique shape and flavorful broth inside. People usually eat khinkali by hand, holding the top knot and biting carefully to enjoy the tasty juice inside. Khinkali is a must-try when experiencing Georgian cuisine.

Traditional Khachapuri is a classic Georgian cheese-filled bread, loved by many. It’s made from soft dough and stuffed with fresh, melted cheese, often with an egg added on top when served. Khachapuri is warm, cheesy, and perfect for sharing with family and friends. It’s one of the most famous and delicious dishes in Georgian cuisine.

Churchkhela is a traditional Georgian sweet treat made by dipping strings of nuts into thickened grape juice and drying them until they become chewy and flavorful. Often called the “Georgian Snickers,” Churchkhela is enjoyed during festivals and special occasions as a natural and delicious dessert.

Georgian dance is a vibrant and expressive art form that reflects the country’s rich history and culture. Known for its energetic moves, precise footwork, and colorful costumes, Georgian dance tells stories of bravery, love, and tradition. Each region of Georgia has its unique style, making these dances diverse and captivating to watch..
